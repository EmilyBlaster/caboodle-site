## APPROACH

**Page hero:** I observe before I design
**Sub:** Most training fails because it's built on assumptions. At Caboodle, I study what people actually do, then design learning experiences that bridge the gap between current and desired behavior.

**"How Every Engagement Unfolds" — the 5-phase process:**
1. **Discover** — Uncover the real business problem and audience needs
2. **Analyze** — Map critical actions to measurable outcomes
3. **Design** — Craft practice-first experiences, not slide decks
4. **Build** — Develop and iterate with rapid prototyping
5. **Measure** — Prove behavior change, not just completion

**"Design with Action Mapping. Measure with Kirkpatrick." table:**

| # | Action Mapping | Kirkpatrick Level | Client Proof |
|---|---|---|---|
| 01 | **Business Goal** — What outcome must change? | **Results (L4)** — Did it matter? | **Apple** — 95% completion across 251 suppliers in 31 countries. The business goal was 95% supplier compliance across 31 countries. We mapped every training element back to this measurable outcome, not seat time. |
| 02 | **Critical Actions** — What must people do differently? | **Behavior (L3)** — Are they doing it? | **Intuit** — 20% boost in retention rates, pharma marketing onboarding. We identified the 5 manager behaviors that predicted internal mobility. Training focused exclusively on rehearsing those actions; everything else was cut. |
| 03 | **Practice Activities** — How do they rehearse it? | **Learning (L2)** — Did they learn it? | **T-Mobile** — 100% completion rate, Legal Affairs compliance training. T-Mobile's Legal Affairs team practiced real compliance scenarios in branching simulations, not multiple-choice quizzes. 100% completion, because it felt relevant. |
| 04 | **Essential Info** — What's the minimum to act? | **Reaction (L1)** — Did they like it? | **PPFA / Bayer** — 180M women reached through patient education materials. We distilled complex medical guidance into the minimum essential information patients needed to act, reaching 180M women worldwide. |

**Closing line:** Most training measures completion. I measure change.

**References:** Action Mapping by Cathy Moore · Kirkpatrick Model by Dr. Donald Kirkpatrick

### /labs/emotion-feedback — Emotion-Aware Feedback · INTERACTIVE (live prototype)
**Tagline:** Same response. Different emotional state. Completely different coaching.
**Framing:** Routes AI-generated coaching through 3 distinct patterns based on self-reported emotional state. Based on Anthropic's April 2026 interpretability research on functional emotion in LLMs.
**Mechanic:** Submit scenario response → pick feeling (struggling / okay / solid) → see 3 coaching responses. Submit 3× to trigger desperation-detection override.
**Example scenario:** AI tool usage with unreliable output before a client call.
**Pull quote:** "When desperation activates, ethical behavior decreases. When calm activates, it recovers." — Anthropic, 2026
**4 L&D takeaways:**
1. Feedback timing changes with state
2. One-size coaching fails measurably
3. Self-report is a viable input
4. (future) Real-time state sensing via behavioral signals
**Limitations / roadmap:** real-time state sensing · longitudinal tracking · multi-turn coaching · LMS integration

my story

training is the last resort,
not the first.

01

I watch first.

Before I design anything, I study how people actually behave at work. I watch. I listen. I ask the uncomfortable questions. Because the gap between what we trained them to do and what they actually do is where the real design challenge lives.

02

I measure change, not completion.

I started Caboodle Design because I was tired of seeing organizations pour resources into training that checked a box but never changed a behavior. My work is built on Kirkpatrick, Action Mapping, and ethnographic research applied anthropologically, not academically.

03

AI is a field tool, not a shortcut.

My work sits at the intersection of behavioral science, media production, and instructional strategy. AI shows up in the workflow as an instrument, never a replacement for the design thinking. The thinking is the job.
